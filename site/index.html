The formula

The carbon footprint is calculated by estimating the energy drawn by the algorithm and the carbon intensity of producing this energy at a given location:

carbon footprint = energy needed × carbon intensity

where the energy needed is:

runtime × (power draw for cores × usage + power draw for memory) × PUE × PSF

The power draw for the computing cores depends on the model and number of cores, while the memory power draw only depends on the size of memory available. The usage factor corrects for the real core usage (default is 1, i.e. full usage). The PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness) measures how much extra energy is needed to operate the data centre (cooling, lighting etc.). The PSF (Pragmatic Scaling Factor) can be used to take into account multiple identical runs (e.g. for testing or optimisation).

Carbon intensity varies more than 100-fold between countries; the same job can emit far less CO2e simply by running on low-carbon electricity.

About CO2e

"Carbon dioxide equivalent" (CO2e) measures the total greenhouse gas emissions of an activity, expressed as the amount of CO2 with the same global-warming potential. It accounts for other gases such as methane or nitrous oxide.

What is a tree-month?

It's the amount of CO2 sequestered by a mature tree in a month (about 917 g CO2). We use it to put carbon emissions in perspective: how long would a tree need to absorb them?

What can you do about it?

The main factors are the location of the computation (carbon intensity of electricity), the hardware efficiency, and how often analyses are (re)run. Choosing greener data centres, energy-efficient hardware, and avoiding unnecessary re-runs can cut emissions dramatically. This website itself was rebuilt as a static page to reduce its own footprint — see the environmental impact of this site.
